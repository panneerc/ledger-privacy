Ledger — Privacy Policy
Last updated: July 4, 2026
Ledger ("the app") is developed and maintained by an individual developer
(contact: panneer9664@gmail.com). This policy explains what information the
app collects, how it's used, and the choices you have.
Information we collect
Account information. When you create an account, we collect the email
address and password you provide. Passwords are handled entirely by our
backend provider, Supabase, using industry-standard hashing — we never see
or store your password in plain text.
Financial data you enter. Ledger is a personal budgeting tool. Any data
you enter — spending categories and limits, transactions, loan/credit card
balances and due dates, and savings goals — is stored so the app can show it
back to you across sessions and devices. We do not collect this data for any
purpose other than operating the app's core features (showing your budget,
calculating totals, and sending you the reminders described below).
We do not collect: your device's contacts, photos, location, browsing
history, or any data from other apps. Ledger does not connect to your bank,
card, or brokerage accounts — all financial figures are manually entered by
you.
How your data is stored
Your account and budgeting data are stored in a Postgres database hosted by
Supabase, Inc., our backend infrastructure provider. Every table is
protected by row-level security rules that mathematically restrict each
account to reading and writing only its own data — there is no
administrative view, dashboard, or export that shows one user's data to
another user, and app support staff (there are none beyond the developer)
cannot browse your data outside of directly querying the database for
support purposes if you ask for help.
Supabase acts as our data processor and maintains its own security and
compliance practices; see Supabase's privacy policy at
https://supabase.com/privacy for details on their infrastructure.
Notifications
Ledger uses on-device local notifications (via Apple's and Google's
notification frameworks) to remind you about category spending limits,
credit utilization, and upcoming debt payments. These notifications are
scheduled entirely on your device based on your own data — no notification
content is sent to or generated by a remote server, and no third party
(including us) is notified when a reminder fires.
Biometric / passcode app lock
If you enable device-level Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint, or passcode
unlock, that authentication is handled entirely by your device's operating
system. Ledger never receives, stores, or transmits your biometric data or
passcode — the app only receives a yes/no "authentication succeeded" result
from the OS.
Data sharing
We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers, data
brokers, or analytics companies. We do not run ads in Ledger. Your data is
shared only with:
Supabase, as the infrastructure provider hosting the database (a data
processor, not an independent user of your data).
Law enforcement or regulators, only if legally compelled to do so.
Your choices and rights
You can review, edit, or delete any category, transaction, debt, or goal
directly in the app at any time. To delete your account and all associated
data entirely, email panneer9664@gmail.com from the address associated with
your account and we will delete your records from the database within 30
days. If you're in a jurisdiction with statutory data rights (e.g. GDPR,
CCPA), those rights — access, correction, deletion, portability — are
honored through the same request process.
Children's privacy
Ledger is not directed at children under 13 (or the relevant minimum age in
your country), and we do not knowingly collect data from children. If you
believe a child has created an account, contact us and we'll delete it.
Changes to this policy
If this policy changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date above. Since
Ledger doesn't have an in-app notification system for policy changes, we
recommend checking this page periodically if you have concerns.
